Rocket launch sites, also known as spaceports or cosmodromes, are specialized facilities designed for the launch and sometimes landing of rockets. These sites are strategically located to optimize launch trajectories, safety, and mission objectives. Here's an overview of notable rocket launch sites around the world: North America Cape Canaveral Space Force Station (CCSFS), Florida, USA A historic launch site active since 1949, CCSFS has hosted numerous military and civilian missions, including early human spaceflights. Kennedy Space Center (KSC), Florida, USA Adjacent to CCSFS, KSC has been operational since 1962 and is NASA's primary launch center for human spaceflight, including the Apollo and Space Shuttle programs. Vandenberg Space Force Base, California, USA Active since 1958, this site is primarily used for polar orbit launches, including satellite deployments and intercontinental ballistic missile tests. Wallops Flight Facility, Virginia, USA Established in 1945, Wallops supports suborbital and orbital launches, serving both NASA and commercial entities. Pacific Spaceport Complex, Kodiak, Alaska, USA Operational since 1991, this site supports satellite launches and missile defense tests. SpaceX Starbase, Boca Chica, Texas, USA Established in 2018, Starbase is SpaceX's private launch site for testing and launching the Starship spacecraft. Europe Guiana Space Centre, Kourou, French Guiana Operational since 1968, this European spaceport is located near the equator, providing an ideal location for geostationary launches. Plesetsk Cosmodrome, Russia Active since 1966, Plesetsk is primarily used for military and commercial satellite launches into polar and sun-synchronous orbits. Baikonur Cosmodrome, Kazakhstan The world's first and largest operational space launch facility, Baikonur has been active since 1957 and is known for launching the first human, Yuri Gagarin, into space. Asia Tanegashima Space Center, Japan Established in 1969, this is Japan's largest rocket-launch complex, handling satellite launches and interplanetary missions. Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota, India Operational since 1971, this is the primary launch center for the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), supporting a range of satellite and exploratory missions. Jiuquan Satellite Launch Center, China Active since 1958, Jiuquan is China's oldest launch facility, supporting both crewed and uncrewed missions. Oceania Rocket Lab Launch Complex 1, Mahia Peninsula, New Zealand Operational since 2017, this private launch site is used by Rocket Lab for launching small satellites into orbit. Africa Broglio Space Centre, Malindi, Kenya Established in the 1960s, this sea-based platform has been used for launching satellites into equatorial orbits. These launch sites play pivotal roles in global space exploration, satellite deployment, and scientific research.
